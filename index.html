CSJS Library

Welcome to CSJS library


Welcome to the CSJS Library, a place where curiosity is a compass and knowledge is an open door. We're thrilled to have you join our community, and we invite you to explore the rich resources we've curated just for you. Our shelves hold a vast and varied collection, from classic literature to the latest non-fiction, and our librarians are always ready to help you discover your next great read. Beyond the physical books, our digital library offers a world of possibilities, with access to countless e-books, audiobooks, academic journals, and streaming services you can enjoy from anywhere.


Whether you're a student seeking a quiet corner for focused study, a researcher digging into a new topic, or a community member simply looking for a new story to get lost in, the CSJS Library is your space. We offer comfortable reading areas, reliable Wi-Fi, and a peaceful atmosphere designed to help you learn, think, and create.

Our goal is to be more than just a place to check out books; we want to be a hub for learning and a source of inspiration for everyone. We regularly host workshops, author talks, and community events, so be sure to check our calendar and get involved.

So, let your curiosity be your guide. We're delighted to welcome you and wish you a wonderful journey of discovery with us.

FINES AND PENALTIES FOR OVERDUE


Library materials returned late are subject to overdue, fines and penalties.

  • First offense - Confiscation of library card, suspension of library privileges for one (1) week plus twenty pesos (Php 20.00) fine.
  • Second offense - Confiscation of library card two weeks suspension of library preveleges. A library fine of twenty pesos (20.00) for the first day and additional fine for the succeeding day will ten pesos (Php 10.00).
  • Third offense - confiscaton of library card, three weeks of suspension of library privileges plus a fine of twenty pesos (Php 20.00) and additional fine for the succeeding day will ten pesos(10.00).


    • RULES AND REGULATIONS


    1. Library users should always present their SCHOOL ID upon entering the library and while in the premises.
    2. Sign the log book when visiting the library.
    3. Always deposit your bags, envelopes at the baggage area. Except the valuable things such as (cash, jewelry, cellphone etc.) the library is not responsible for any loss or damage to your property.
    4. Student's service shall cease 15 minutes before the indicted closing time to be able the library staff to put the library in order for the next day's work.
    5. Present validated ID when borrowing books and any library materials.
    6. Reserved books, general references, periodical material, and other special library collections are strictly for library use only.


    STUDENT CONDUCT INSIDE THE LIBRARY


    1. Proper conduct is expected of all users at all times. Unnecessary noise, smoking, eating, sleeping and defacing library furniture and equipment in the library premises is strictly prohibited. Any library staff on-duty has the authority to eject anyone causing disturbances.
    2. Seats are not to be reserved and rearranged. Anything left on the reading table for this purpose shall be removed by the library staff to make the room for other clientele.
    3. Only notebooks needed for study research may be brought inside the library. Students may bring their personal books shall present to the library staff on duties and subject for inspections after its use.
    4. Violations of the rules of conduct shall be reported to the student's affair office for the proper disposition.
    5. Return the books to their proper places.
    6. Return the seats to their proper places before leaving.
    7. Mutilation and stealing of library materials, writing on books and other library reading materials, tables, chairs, tearing of pages, theft of any library materials and all other forms of mutilation beyond resonable are strictly prohibited and subject to disciplinary action. The penalty shall be prescribed by the students affairs office to a report shall be sent by the librarian.
    8. Should need any help, don't hesitate to approach your very friendly staff on duty.


    SUPPLEMENTARY READING MATERIALS


    Magazines, journals, newspaper and any other similar publications are called periodicals. they gotthis name beacause they issue at definite periods of time (daily, weekly, and monthly).



    THE USE OF CARD CATALOGUE


    In order to facilatate the search for books in the library, the first thing to do is to consult the card catalogue. Copy the call number of the needed book and look for it on the book shelves. Books are arranged alphabetically according to the first word in the firs line of each catalog.
